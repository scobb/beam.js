Beam vs Plausible

Plausible is the most popular Google Analytics alternative. Fully open source (AGPL-3), EU-hosted, strong community.

Where Beam wins: Price ($5/mo vs $9/mo), free tier (50K pageviews/mo vs none), Cloudflare global edge vs single EU region.

Where Plausible wins: Fully open source (self-hostable), larger community, better documentation, more integrations, founded 2019 vs 2026.

Bottom line: Choose Beam for price and free tier. Choose Plausible for open source guarantees or EU-only data storage.

Beam vs Fathom

Fathom is premium privacy analytics with EU-isolated infrastructure and a strong reputation for simplicity.

Where Beam wins: Price ($5/mo vs $14/mo — 64% cheaper), free tier available, open-source script.

Where Fathom wins: EU-isolated infrastructure (legal guarantee, not just technical), simpler pricing (unlimited sites on all plans), stronger brand trust.

Bottom line: Choose Beam for affordability. Choose Fathom if EU data isolation is a legal or compliance requirement.

Beam vs Umami

Umami is free and fully open source (MIT). You host it on your own server — complete data ownership.

Where Beam wins: No server required (managed hosting included), zero infrastructure cost on free tier, automatic backups and global CDN.

Where Umami wins: Completely free if self-hosted, full data ownership, fork-and-modify freedom, Umami Cloud at $9/mo.

Bottom line: Choose Beam if you don't want to manage infrastructure. Choose Umami for full control and maximum cost savings.
